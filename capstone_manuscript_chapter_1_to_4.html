CHAPTER 1

INTRODUCTION

Small-scale shoe cleaning, restoration, and other service-oriented establishments play an important role in local economic activity, particularly in urban areas where they provide accessible products and services to the community. However, many of these businesses still depend on manual or semi-digital processes in managing their operations. The lack of integrated systems for handling transactions, inventory, and customer data often results in inefficiencies, inaccurate records, and limited capacity for business analysis. In an increasingly data-driven environment, the inability to utilize advanced technologies such as web-based platforms and data analytics puts small enterprises at a competitive disadvantage.

In the case of Ms. Aileen Rosales, owner of Shoelotskey, located in Villamor, Pasay City, operational challenges are evident in the management of services, sales transactions, and inventory. The current process relies heavily on manual recording, making it difficult to track customer purchases, monitor stock levels, and organize service-related data efficiently. This results in inconsistencies in records, delays in retrieving information, and challenges in identifying trends such as frequently purchased services or peak sales periods. Consequently, decision-making is often based on assumptions rather than data-driven insights, limiting the business's ability to optimize its operations and improve customer service.

Despite the growing availability of web-based management systems and machine learning technologies, there remains a significant research gap in developing integrated solutions specifically tailored for small-scale establishments like Shoelotskey. Existing systems are often designed for large enterprises, making them complex, costly, and unsuitable for businesses with limited resources and technical expertise. Moreover, many studies focus either on basic automation or on advanced analytics independently, with minimal emphasis on combining service management systems and machine learning-based data analytics into a single, practical solution for small businesses.

To address these challenges, this study proposes the development of a web-based service management system integrated with data analytics using machine learning algorithms for Shoelotskey. The proposed system aims to automate core business processes such as service and sales management, inventory tracking, and report generation while incorporating machine learning techniques to analyze historical data and generate predictive insights. These insights may include sales trends and predicted service completion times. By implementing this system, the business is expected to enhance operational efficiency, improve accuracy, and support data-driven decision-making. Ultimately, this research seeks to provide an innovative yet practical solution that bridges the gap between advanced technology and the operational needs of small-scale establishments.

Problem Statement

Shoelotskey currently experiences specific operational challenges stemming from its reliance on manual processes. Based on preliminary observations and interviews with the business owner, the following problems have been identified:

  • Service records, customer information, and inventory data are recorded manually or maintained in separate files. This fragmentation makes data retrieval, updates, and monitoring time-consuming and error-prone.
  • The business lacks a centralized dashboard for monitoring daily operations. Business owner cannot easily access information about pending services, completed transactions, service demand trends, or sales performance.
  • Service completion date estimation depends entirely on staff judgment rather than historical data analysis. This results in inconsistent estimates and potential customer dissatisfaction.
  • Report preparation requires significant manual effort. Daily, weekly, monthly, quarterly, and annually sales and performance summaries must be compiled manually, reducing administrative productivity.

According to preliminary data collected from Shoelotskey operations:

  • Average service processing delays due to manual status updates: 2-3 days
  • Current manual report preparation time: approximately 4-6 hours per month
  • Record retrieval time for customer history: 15-30 minutes per request
  • Monthly average transaction volume: 280-300 service orders

Without a structured system and data-driven approach, the business faces continued operational inefficiency and limited capacity for growth.

Research Questions

This study seeks to answer the following questions:

  1. How can a web-based service management system be designed and implemented for Shoelotskey to centralize service transactions, customer records, and inventory monitoring?
  2. How can machine learning algorithms be applied to predict service completion dates using historical service records and workload data?
  3. How can data analytics features be integrated and evaluated to provide meaningful insights regarding sales trends, service demand, and business performance?
  4. To what extent does the developed system meet ISO/IEC 25010 software quality standards in terms of: 4.1 Functional suitability, 4.2 Performance efficiency, 4.3 Usability, 4.4 Reliability, 4.5 Security, 4.6 Maintainability, and 4.7 Portability?
  5. What is the level of user acceptance of the developed system based on the Technology Acceptance Model (TAM) in terms of: 5.1 Perceived usefulness, 5.2 Perceived ease of use, 5.3 Behavioral intention to use, 5.4 Actual system use?
  6. What improvements may be recommended for future enhancement of the system?

Project Context

Micro, small, and medium enterprises (MSMEs) play a significant role in economic development by generating employment opportunities, supporting local commerce, and providing accessible products and services to surrounding communities. In the Philippines, many small service-oriented businesses continue to rely on manual or semi-digital operational processes such as handwritten logbooks, spreadsheet records, and fragmented paper-based documentation. While these traditional practices may be manageable at a small scale, they often become inefficient as transaction volume increases. Common issues include delayed retrieval of records, inaccurate inventory counts, difficulty monitoring service progress, and limited access to timely business information.

The growing use of digital technologies has transformed how businesses manage operations, analyze performance, and improve customer service. Web-based information systems enable centralized data storage, real-time monitoring, automated reporting, and faster transaction processing. Likewise, business analytics tools allow owners and managers to identify sales trends, customer demand patterns, operational bottlenecks, and performance opportunities. Businesses that fail to adopt these technologies may experience slower decision-making, higher administrative workload, and reduced competitiveness.

Shoelotskey, a shoe cleaning and restoration service business located in Villamor, Pasay City, owned by Ms. Aileen Rosales, currently experiences operational challenges associated with manual record management. Based on initial interviews and observation, service transactions, inventory usage, customer records, and service status updates are recorded manually or through isolated files. This setup makes it difficult to monitor pending and completed services, track frequently availed services, prepare sales summaries, and estimate completion schedules accurately. As a result, managerial decisions may rely more on assumptions than on organized operational data.

Another operational challenge involves predicting service completion dates. Customers commonly expect clear estimates regarding release schedules; however, manual estimation based only on staff experience may produce inconsistent results. Inaccurate estimates may lead to delays, customer dissatisfaction, and inefficient workload distribution. Predictive technologies such as machine learning can help address this issue by learning patterns from historical service data and generating more reliable completion estimates.

Project Purpose and Description

This study aims to develop and evaluate a Web-Based Service Management System with Analytics and Machine Learning for Shoelotskey, a shoe cleaning and restoration service business located in Villamor, Pasay City. The study seeks to address operational issues such as manual record-keeping, fragmented files, delayed reporting, limited-service monitoring, and inconsistent estimation of service completion dates.

The proposed system is intended to centralize customer records, job orders, inventory data, service transactions, and reporting processes through a web-based platform accessible to authorized users. It includes modules for order entry, inventory monitoring, service status tracking, sales reporting, dashboard analytics, and machine learning-based completion date estimation.

System quality will be assessed using ISO/IEC 25010 standards, while user acceptance will be measured using the Technology Acceptance Model (TAM). The system is intended for internal use by Shoelotskey management and staff only.

Machine Learning for Service Completion Time Prediction

Accurate service completion estimates remain elusive when based on intuition alone. Machine learning offers a data-driven alternative, but applicability depends heavily on dataset size and model complexity. Caturkusuma et al. [15] achieved an R² of 0.98 with Random Forest on IT incident data, while Cribillero et al. [19] demonstrated >80% accuracy with Random Forest and SVM in small businesses.

This pragmatic, context-specific design overcomes the scalability critique leveled at enterprise-focused work and directly answers Research Question 2.

CHAPTER 3

TECHNICAL BACKGROUND

Web-based information systems are widely used by service-oriented businesses to manage daily operations such as job orders, customer transactions, service records, scheduling, and report generation. In this study, a Web-Based Service Management System with Data Analytics using Machine Learning Algorithms was developed for Shoelotskey to improve service transaction management, monitor service progress, and generate operational reports through a centralized digital platform.

The proposed system follows a three-tier architecture, consisting of the presentation layer, application layer, and data layer. This architecture was selected because it separates the user interface, business logic, and database operations into organized components, making the system easier to maintain, troubleshoot, and expand.

The presentation layer is the front-end component accessed by users through standard web browsers. It was developed using React with TypeScript to ensure structured, reusable, and maintainable code. Vite was utilized as the build tool to provide fast development performance and optimized application loading. For the user interface, Tailwind CSS was implemented to create a clean, responsive, and user-friendly design across desktop and mobile devices. Lucide React was also integrated to provide consistent and professional icons throughout the system.

The application layer contains the core system logic and server-side processing. It was developed using Python with the FastAPI framework, which was selected because of its high performance, lightweight structure, and ability to efficiently handle multiple user requests. FastAPI also supports RESTful API development, asynchronous processing, and seamless integration with analytical modules.

The data layer uses PostgreSQL, a robust open-source relational database management system used for storing and managing operational data. PostgreSQL was selected because of its reliability, scalability, advanced querying features, and strong security controls. SQLAlchemy ORM was used to simplify database transactions and improve maintainability of database operations.

The machine learning module is integrated into the system to predict estimated service completion timelines based on historical transaction data. The module was developed using the Scikit-Learn library in Python. A Random Forest Regression model was selected because the required output is a continuous numerical value, specifically the predicted number of days or completion date. This algorithm was chosen because of its strong predictive accuracy, ability to handle nonlinear relationships, resistance to overfitting, and suitability for structured business datasets.

Software Development Life Cycle (SDLC)

The development of the Web-Based Service Management System adopted the Waterfall Software Development Life Cycle (SDLC) model. This model was selected because it follows a systematic and phase-by-phase process that is appropriate for academic and capstone projects requiring clear documentation, defined outputs, and controlled implementation. Each development stage must be completed before proceeding to the next.

System Security and Data Privacy Design

The System Security and Data Privacy Design of the proposed Web-Based Service Management System with Data Analytics using Machine Learning Algorithms for Shoelotskey aims to ensure the protection, confidentiality, and integrity of operational data stored and processed within the system. Since the system manages sensitive business information, including job orders, service transactions, customer information, and user credentials, appropriate security measures are implemented to prevent unauthorized access, data breaches, and misuse of the system.

The system's security framework integrates multiple protection mechanisms, including authentication controls, role-based access management, encryption protocols (e.g., Bcrypt hashing algorithm), and JWT validation, adhering to Top 10 OWASP security standards to actively intercept and monitor unauthorized API accesses and SQL injection attempts.

Data Privacy and Ethical Considerations

The proposed system is designed to follow responsible data privacy and ethical data handling practices to protect the privacy of individuals whose information may be recorded during service transactions. Customer information and service records are treated as confidential data and are stored securely within the system database.

Access to operational data is restricted only to authorized users based on their assigned roles within the system. This restriction ensures that sensitive information cannot be accessed, shared, or modified by unauthorized individuals. All data collected by the system is used exclusively for service management, operational monitoring, and business reporting purposes.

Audit Logging and Activity Monitoring

To maintain transparency and accountability within system operations, the proposed platform incorporates audit logging and activity monitoring mechanisms. Audit logs record significant system activities performed by users and system processes.

The system logs events such as user authentication attempts, job order creation, service status updates, report generation, and user account modifications. Each log entry includes relevant details, such as the user responsible for the action, the activity performed, and the event timestamp.

Implementation Environment and Technology Stack

The implementation environment and technology stack describe the technological infrastructure used to develop, deploy, and operate the proposed web-based system. The system is built using modern web technologies that support efficient application development, secure data management, and reliable system performance.

Software Requirements

The system requires a web server platform capable of hosting the application and processing user requests. Server-side software handles system logic, data processing, and machine-learning analytics to analyze historical service records. A relational database management system is required to store and manage system data, including job orders, service records, transaction details, and user account information.

Hardware Requirements

The hardware requirements for the proposed system include computing devices capable of accessing web-based applications and supporting internet connectivity. The owner and staff members will use desktop or laptop computers equipped with modern web browsers to interact with the system interface.

Development Tools and Programming Technologies

The frontend of the system is built using React with TypeScript to ensure structured and maintainable code. Vite is used to support fast development and efficient system performance, while Tailwind CSS provides a clean, responsive, and user-friendly interface design. Lucide React is integrated to maintain a consistent and professional set of icons.

The backend is developed using Python with the FastAPI framework. This setup allows the system to handle multiple user requests efficiently while enforcing business rules and processing system logic. For data storage, the system uses a PostgreSQL relational database. SQLAlchemy ORM is applied to manage database operations.

The system’s predictive analytics feature is implemented using the Scikit-Learn library. A Random Forest regression model is used to estimate service completion timelines based on historical service data.

Deployment Environment

The proposed system is deployed within a secure, cloud-based web environment. For production, the system is hosted on the Heroku cloud platform, where both frontend and backend components are deployed using dedicated dynos to ensure stable and efficient execution. The application’s database is managed via Heroku Postgres, which provides high availability, automated backups, and secure database connections.

CHAPTER 4

RESEARCH METHODOLOGY, RESULTS, AND DISCUSSIONS

Research Design

This study used a developmental-descriptive research design. It focused on the design, development, and evaluation of a Web-Based Service Management System with Data Analytics using Machine Learning Algorithms. The developmental aspect was used to create the proposed system, while the descriptive aspect was applied to assess its functionality, usability, and relevance to the business operations. The study followed the Waterfall Software Development Life Cycle (SDLC), which includes requirements gathering, system design, development, testing, deployment, and maintenance.

Data Gathering Technique

The researchers gathered data through interviews, focus group discussions, observation, and document review. Interviews were conducted with the owner and staff of Shoelotskey to identify current problems, operational challenges, and service management system requirements. A focus group discussion was also conducted to allow the participants to collectively share their experiences, concerns, and suggestions regarding the existing manual process and the features needed in the proposed system. Observation was used to examine the business's actual workflow, service transactions, and record-keeping practices.

Statistical Treatment of Data

The data gathered from the evaluation of the proposed system were analyzed using descriptive statistics, particularly the weighted mean, to determine the level of acceptability of the system in terms of functionality, usability, reliability, efficiency, and security. The weighted mean was used to summarize and interpret the responses from the respondents based on the evaluation questionnaire.

In addition, the study applied regression analysis as part of the machine learning component to develop a service completion date prediction model. Historical service data were processed using Python with scikit-learn to identify patterns and generate predictions regarding expected service completion dates. The predictive performance of the model was evaluated using Mean Absolute Error (MAE).

Population and Sampling Technique

The respondents of this study will consist of individuals directly involved in the operation and evaluation of the proposed system for Shoelotskey. The target population includes the business owner and five employees who are responsible for customer transactions, service processing, shoe cleaning and restoration services, and inventory-related tasks.

In addition to the internal respondents, the study will include selected Information Technology (IT) experts to evaluate the technical quality of the system based on ISO/IEC 25010 standards. These experts may include IT instructors, software developers, system analysts, programmers, or professionals with experience in software evaluation and system development.

The study will use total population sampling for the Shoelotskey staff and management since the number of internal respondents is small and manageable. This allows all employees and management personnel to participate in evaluating the system. For the IT experts, purposive sampling will be used to select qualified evaluators with relevant technical knowledge and experience in software development and quality assessment.
